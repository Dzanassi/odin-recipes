The Best Cheesecake Bars!

for this recipe you will need:


Raspberry Sauce

  1. 2 teaspoons room-temperature water, divided
  2. 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  3. 1 and 1/2 cups (205g) fresh or frozen raspberries (do not thaw)
  4. 2 Tablespoons (25g) granulated sugar

Oreo Crust

  1. 20 Oreos
  2. 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

White Chocolate Cheesecake Filling

  1. 6 ounces (170g) white chocolate, finely chopped
  2. 2 8-ounce blocks (16 oz; 450g) full-fat cream cheese, softened to room temperature
  3. 1/3 cup (65g) granulated sugar
  4. 1 Tablespoon (8g) all-purpose flour
  5. 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  6. 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  7. 1/8 teaspoon salt
  8. 2 large eggs, at room temperature

Step by step recipe

Raspberry Sauce:
  1. Mix some water and cornstarch together.
  2. Cook fresh or frozen raspberries, sugar, and more water together on the stovetop.
  3. Add cornstarch mixture.
  4. Once slightly thickened, remove from heat.
  5. Press raspberries through a fine mesh strainer to rid the seeds. Discard the seeds.
  6. Let the seedless sauce cool completely before using.

Oreo Crust

  1. Using a food processor or blender, pulse the Oreos (the whole Oreo—cookie AND filling) into crumbs.
  2. Mix with melted butter.
  3. Press into a lined 9-inch square baking pan.
  4. Pre-bake for 8 minutes as you prepare the filling.

Cheese Cake Filling

  1. Chop and melt pure white chocolate.
  2. Don’t use white chocolate chips/morsels. Instead, pick up two 4-ounce white chocolate baking bars from the baking aisle; I prefer Ghirardelli or Baker’s brands sold right next to the chocolate chips. You need 6 ounces for this recipe. Cool the white chocolate as you make the batter.
  3. As the white chocolate cools, beat two 8-ounce blocks of cream cheese with some sugar.
  4. Add flour, lemon juice to brighten up the otherwise flat flavor (don’t worry, you don’t taste the lemon), vanilla extract, and salt.
  5. Beat in 2 eggs.
  6. Add melted/cooled white chocolate.

Bringing it all together:

  1. Pour 1/2 of the cheesecake filling onto warm pre-baked Oreo cookie crust.
  2. Drizzle with 1/2 of the cooled raspberry sauce.
  3. Pour remaining 1/2 of the cheesecake filling on top.
  4. Drizzle with remaining raspberry sauce.
  5. Swirl everything together with a toothpick.
  6. Bake! No water bath required. The bars take about 32–36 minutes in the oven. They’ll puff up as they bake, then settle back down as they cool.
